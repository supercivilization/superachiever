## `/superpowers` — Superpowers · **NEW**

**(a) Intention.** Make the achiever *feel* they can author their doing instead of being at its mercy: the loop is the DOING engine that turns intent into finished results. Show the harness — how the loop becomes runnable and hand-off-able. Pairs with `/supermind`. End state: *"I want this loop running for me."*

**(b) Content — the researched substance (from the Supermind-Superpowers brief).** Superpowers are not motivation — they are **the loop made runnable**: Current → Desired → Actions → Results, wired so agents, tools, and systems carry it. The definitive, ownable claim: *most productivity advice optimizes one segment and calls it the whole system; the superpower is the **complete** loop — and the three named failure points let you diagnose exactly where you're stuck.*
- **Starting — Current → Desired.** Name what you don't want precisely enough to define what you do want; **desire is contrast, not fantasy.** Substance: Oettingen's **mental contrasting** — positive fantasy *alone* predicts *worse* outcomes (less weight lost, weaker job-search results); holding the desired future *and* the present obstacle together is what converts a wish into energized commitment. Boyd's OODA: **Orient** is the pivot — see your Current accurately before deciding.
- **Focusing — Desired → Actions.** The few next steps, chosen and triggered — leverage, not busywork. Substance: Gollwitzer's **implementation intentions** ("if X, then I'll do Y") roughly **double-to-triple** follow-through — a **medium-large effect (d ≈ 0.65) across 94 studies**, confirmed by a 2024 mega-analysis over 642 tests. Allen's **"next action"** (GTD); price the actions with the **outside view** (planning-fallacy correction), not the inside view.
- **Finishing — Actions → Results.** Run to a **measured** result; close the loop instead of abandoning it. Substance: the **intention–behavior gap** — only ~**47%** of intentions become action (Sheeran); the **goal-gradient effect** (Kivetz: a 12-stamp card with 2 free stamps finished a 10-purchase goal in a median 10 vs. 15 days) — engineer visible progress; **Zeigarnik** — every unclosed loop taxes attention, so finishing is how you get your mind back.
- **What runs it** — agents (do the work), tools (extend the reach), systems (make it repeat). Plain examples, no product names — the point is *delegation done well*.
- **The two moments that stay yours** — set the Desired, authorize the Actions; everything between is delegable → bridge to `/supermind`.

**(c) Design.** Page chrome = **violet→purple** (hero rule `from-violet-500 to-purple-500`, eyebrow tint). **The loop itself = the Genius-Loop 4-color engine:** render Current→Desired→Actions→Results as four nodes tinted `mauve · mist · olive · taupe`, connected by the three seams as gradients (Starting mauve→mist, Focusing mist→olive, Finishing olive→taupe) — the same bordered grid as home, color-anchored, mono step numbers. "What runs it" = 3-item list, violet dots. Minimal motion (reduced-motion honored). A subtle "last refined" / loop-completion motif practices the finishing principle it teaches.

**(d) StoryBrand.** Empathy: "you're not lazy or scattered — half of all intentions never become action; that's default human wiring." Authority: one clean loop, three named moves, each backed by the strongest finding in goal science. The diagnostic — *"which of the three are you stuck at right now?"* — is the reusable hook.

Gloss on first use: *the three things a good life actually runs on — your health, your money, your peace — treated as one system.*
- **Health & Energy** *(amber-500)* — "The energy everything else runs on." **Healthspan, not lifespan** (Attia's Medicine 3.0 vs the reactive Medicine 2.0; the **Four Horsemen** — cardiovascular, cancer, neurodegenerative, metabolic — are largely delayable). The most-cited lever: **cardiorespiratory fitness (VO₂max)** — every 1-MET gain ≈ **11–17% lower all-cause mortality**; higher midlife VO₂max linked to ~50% lower mortality. Strength/muscle protect independence. Planning tool: the **Centenarian Decathlon**. Credibility guard: cite the Blue Zones *habits*, not the debunked supercentenarian *data*.
- **Wealth & Career** *(amber-400 / yellow-500)* — "Money as a tool, not a scoreboard." **Financial independence** = investment income covers living costs; the **4% / 25× rule** (Bengen 1994, Trinity Study) as a planning anchor — honestly, ~3.5–4.7% given today's valuations. Housel: *"doing well with money is about how you behave"* — **savings rate > income > returns**, compounding is the whole game, **wealth is what you don't see**, and **know your "enough"** (the bridge to Peace). Career income is the engine that funds the savings rate.
- **Peace & People** *(yellow-400)* — "The relationships and calm that make it worth it." The **Harvard Study of Adult Development** (85+ years): **the quality of your close relationships is the single best predictor of a happy — and healthy — life** (warmer connections → less heart disease/diabetes, slower cognitive decline). The resolved money-happiness finding (Killingsworth–Kahneman 2023): happiness keeps rising with income for most people — the old "$75k plateau" was overturned. **PERMA** as a practical checklist of well-being levers.
- **How the office carries it (the integration thesis — our ownable claim)** — energy funds everything; money buys back time and removes a category of stress; relationships are what the other two are *for* and pay back into health. **The failure mode is optimizing one piece to the ruin of the others** (the fit-but-broke, the rich-but-lonely). The discipline is keeping all three above threshold at once — run async, so progress compounds while your attention is elsewhere.

## `/business` — Business Success Puzzle · **NEW**

**(a) Intention.** Make the achiever feel their *enterprise* runs without consuming them — a full operating team handles the market, the ops, and the money; staffed-office leverage they own and approve. Networked-edge register (bridges toward the MFO). End state: *"my business would have a real operating team — owned by me."*

**(b) Content — the researched substance (from the Business-Success-Puzzle brief).** The "office" in family office: a business is not a hustle or a personality — it's **one integrated system with three faces**. Credibility fact: our three sub-items *are* the industry-standard frame — Strategyzer's **Business Model Canvas** literally teaches a **front stage** (value for customers → revenue) and a **back stage** (resources, activities, partners → costs). Gloss: *running your business as one system with three faces.*
- **Front-stage — Users** *(cyan-500)* — where value is experienced. Governed by the **Value Proposition Canvas**: match your offer (products, pain relievers, gain creators) to the customer's **jobs, pains, gains** (Christensen's Jobs-to-be-Done: "people don't buy a drill, they want a hole"). The stake, measured: **"no market need" is the #1 startup-failure reason — 42%** (CB Insights).
- **Back-stage — Admin** *(teal-500)* — where value is made: key activities, resources, partnerships. "Back-stage infrastructure supports the front stage — never the reverse." The under-managed face where quiet failure lives (**~23% of failures trace to not having the right team**). The AI-native turn: for a one-person/family-scale business, the back stage is increasingly *agentic* — activities delegated to named agents with an approval queue and audit trail.
- **Bottom-line — Profit** *(teal-600)* — where the model is proven: revenue streams (front) minus cost structure (back). **Cash is the oxygen** — ~82% of failures trace to cash-flow mismanagement, ~29% to simply running out of cash; profit on paper isn't survival. Base rate (BLS): ~20.4% of new businesses fail in year one, ~49.4% within five years. *The office gives full bottom-line visibility — and never moves your money* (the trust gate, frontstage-plain).
- **How the office carries it** — the three faces constrain each other, so it's a **puzzle, not a checklist**: value users love that costs more to deliver than they'll pay is a charity; a lean back stage delivering something nobody wants is a hobby. The integrator keeps all three coherent — one system you own and authorize, run async. *(The full C-suite — Board/CEO/CVO/COO/CMO/CFO and its color-coding — is the BACKSTAGE rationale, felt as competence, never displayed as a diagram or named in copy.)*

Cool, precise, *seawater-clean competence* — slightly more operational than `/personal`, same restraint. Figures use tabular-nums. Optional signature: a single quiet three-face "theatre" visual (front stage / back stage / lit footlights = bottom line) — high-craft, screenshot-worthy — but only if it earns its place; hairlines and space over boxes otherwise.

**(d) StoryBrand.** Hero = capable AI-native builder, good at *part* of business, feels the rest as fog; the real problem is **incoherence**, not effort. Success: *"you can see the whole board now."*
